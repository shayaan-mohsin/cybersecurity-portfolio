# Executive Summary

## Situation

The organization faces realistic risk from phishing and credential theft. These threats can affect email, cloud applications, vendor access, and sensitive business data.

## Key Findings

- Credential theft remains a practical path into business systems.
- MFA, account review, and login monitoring are core defenses.
- User reporting and response playbooks improve detection and containment.
- Leadership visibility is important because account compromise can affect operations and trust.

## Recommendations

| Recommendation | Priority | Business value |
|---|---|---|
| Enforce MFA | High | Reduces likelihood of unauthorized access |
| Create phishing reporting workflow | High | Speeds detection and escalation |
| Review shared and inactive accounts | High | Reduces persistence risk |
| Monitor suspicious authentication | Medium | Improves visibility |
| Run credential-compromise tabletop | Medium | Improves response coordination |

## Conclusion

The most effective near-term improvements are practical and governance-aligned: MFA, account review, user reporting, monitoring, and response planning. These controls reduce risk while supporting clearer accountability.
