# Risk Register

| ID | Risk | Affected area | Likelihood | Impact | Priority | Recommended response | NIST CSF function |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| R-001 | Inconsistent access review process | Employee and vendor accounts | Medium | High | High | Establish quarterly access reviews and document account ownership | Govern / Protect |
| R-002 | Limited incident response documentation | Operations and leadership | Medium | High | High | Create a lightweight incident response playbook and escalation matrix | Respond / Govern |
| R-003 | Weak vendor access governance | Third-party support | Medium | High | High | Require least privilege, named accounts, and periodic vendor access review | Govern / Protect |
| R-004 | Security awareness is informal | Employees | High | Medium | Medium | Implement annual security awareness and phishing reporting guidance | Protect |
| R-005 | Asset inventory is incomplete | SaaS tools and endpoints | Medium | Medium | Medium | Build a basic asset and application inventory with business owners | Identify |
| R-006 | Backup and recovery expectations are unclear | Critical workflows | Low | High | Medium | Define recovery objectives and test backup restoration process | Recover |

## Summary

The highest-priority risks are access governance, incident response readiness, and third-party access control. These areas create practical risk for a healthcare environment because they affect sensitive data, business continuity, and accountability.
