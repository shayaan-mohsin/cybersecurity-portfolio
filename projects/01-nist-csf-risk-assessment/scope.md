# Scope And Assumptions

## Organization

CareBridge Health Services is a fictional small healthcare services organization used for public portfolio analysis. The assessment is based on realistic but invented organization data and public NIST CSF 2.0 guidance.

This is not a formal audit, HIPAA compliance attestation, penetration test, or legal assessment.

## Assessment Type

Framework-based cybersecurity risk assessment using NIST CSF 2.0 functions:

- Govern
- Identify
- Protect
- Detect
- Respond
- Recover

## In Scope

- Patient scheduling workflow
- Billing support workflow
- Document intake and cloud storage workflow
- Employee account access
- Third-party vendor access
- Basic asset inventory maturity
- Security awareness and incident response readiness
- Basic cloud/SaaS usage

## Out Of Scope

- Live patient data
- Real production systems
- Penetration testing
- Legal compliance attestation
- Vendor-specific audit evidence
- Technical vulnerability scanning
- Security architecture validation
- Contract review

## Assumptions

- The organization handles sensitive patient and business information.
- Several workflows depend on SaaS tools and vendor support.
- Current security documentation is incomplete.
- Leadership wants a practical risk roadmap, not a technical deep dive only.
- The organization has limited internal security staffing.
- Recommendations should be realistic for a small organization with modest cybersecurity maturity.

## Assessment Goal

Identify the most important governance and cyber risk gaps, map them to NIST CSF 2.0 functions, and recommend a prioritized improvement roadmap.

## Data Used

## Method

1. Define the organization profile, sensitive data types, business priorities, and core workflows.
2. Identify realistic risks across access control, vendor access, documentation, monitoring, incident response, awareness, and recovery.
3. Score each risk by likelihood and impact using a simple High / Medium / Low model.
4. Map each risk to NIST CSF 2.0 functions and relevant category themes.
5. Prioritize the roadmap by business value, feasibility, and risk reduction.
6. Write an executive brief that communicates recommendations in plain business language.
