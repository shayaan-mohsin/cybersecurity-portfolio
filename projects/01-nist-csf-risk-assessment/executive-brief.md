# Executive Brief

## Situation

CareBridge Health Services depends on employee accounts, vendor support, SaaS tools, and sensitive healthcare-related workflows. The organization needs a practical security roadmap that improves governance, access control, and incident readiness.

## Key Findings

- Access review practices are inconsistent.
- Vendor access creates risk without strong ownership and periodic review.
- Incident response steps are not clearly documented.
- Asset and SaaS inventory maturity is limited.

## Business Impact

These gaps can affect patient trust, operational continuity, and compliance readiness. The most urgent risks are not only technical; they are governance and accountability risks.

## Recommendations

| Recommendation | Priority | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Establish quarterly access reviews | High | Reduces stale account and privilege risk |
| Create vendor access standards | High | Improves third-party accountability |
| Draft an incident response playbook | High | Improves readiness during security events |
| Build a lightweight asset inventory | Medium | Improves visibility and ownership |
| Run security awareness training | Medium | Reduces preventable user-driven risk |

## Conclusion

CareBridge should prioritize governance basics first: ownership, access review, vendor access, and incident response readiness. These improvements are realistic, measurable, and aligned with NIST CSF 2.0 functions.
